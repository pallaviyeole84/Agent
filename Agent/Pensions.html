PENSION PLAN

Pension plans or retirement plans offer you the dual benefits of investment and insurance cover. It is nothing but investing a certain amount regularly to accumulate over a specific tenure in a phase-by-phase manner. This will ensure a steady flow of monthly pension once you retire. Provident Fund, for example, is a popular retirement planning scheme.

* PENSION PLAN BENEFITS:

1. Provision of Regular Income Post Retirement:

One of the biggest benefits of an pension plan is that it provides incomes after retirement. Pension schemes available in India help you cover your living expenses post retirement by providing a guaranteed income. With the variety of pension plans available out there, you can choose from one which suits your needs best. While some plans provide you with lifelong income, there are others which offer better returns.

2.Tax Benefits :

Pension plans are entitled to tax exemption specified under Section 80C. If you want to contribute to pension plans, the Indian Income Tax Act, 1961, offers significant tax respite under Chapter VI-A. Section 80C, 80CCC and 80CCD specify them in detail. For instance, Atal Pension Yojana (APY) and National Pension Scheme (NPS) are subject to tax deduction under 80CCD.

3.Death Benefit :

In the unfortunate event of the life assured passing away after risk commencement while the policy is in force, the sum assured on death and any accrued bonuses will be paid.

4.Corporation Profits :

Policyholders are eligible to participate in profits earned by LIC, earning bonuses through the same.

5.Surrender Value :

Individuals can surrender the policy (after completion of 3 years) to get an assured surrender value.

6.Rebates :

LIC provides a rebate on high premium amounts, helping one save money.

7.Loan :

One can avail a loan under this policy, helping them cater to emergencies.

* Documents Required For Policy:

1. Self Attested Photograph.
2. Age Proof: (school certificate, passport, PAN card, driving license).
3.Residence proof (ration card, election card, electricity bill, water bill, telephone bill, credit card bill, bank passbook, driving license, rent agreement, passport etc)
4.Photo I D proof (PAN card, D/L, passport, election card, Ration card & bank pass book with the proposers photograph on it).
5. Income proof (salary slip, form 16, Income tax returns, pension pass book etc) (If total Premium Exceeds one lakh).
